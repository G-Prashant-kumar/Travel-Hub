Getting Around London

London has a comprehensive public transportation system, including buses, tubes (subway), trains, and river boats. You can use a single Oyster card or a contactless payment card to pay for fares. Consider purchasing a Visitor Oyster card, which offers discounts and convenience for tourists.

London Buses

With over 8,000 buses, London’s bus network is extensive. You can plan your route using the Transport for London (TfL) website or a journey planner app.

London Underground (Tube)

The Tube has 11 lines, covering much of the city. Be mindful of peak hours (Monday to Friday, 7 am to 9 am and 4 pm to 7 pm) when fares are higher.

Trains

London has several major train stations, including King’s Cross, Victoria, and Paddington. National Rail services connect London to the rest of the UK.

River Boats

Thames Clippers operate a river bus service along the Thames, offering a unique perspective on the city.

Visitor Centres

Two main visitor centres can assist with travel planning:

  • Victoria Visitor Centre: Located opposite Platform 8 at Victoria Rail Station (SW1V 1JU). Open Wednesday to Sunday, 9 am to 4:30 pm.
  • TfL Visitor Centre: At King’s Cross St (N1 9AL). No fixed opening hours, but staff are available to assist.

Other Tips

Check TfL’s website for quieter times to travel and busiest stations/stops.

Familiarize yourself with the London Underground map and learn how to use an Oyster card.

Consider purchasing a London Travelcard for unlimited travel on buses, tubes, and trains.

Plan ahead and book accommodations and attractions in advance to avoid peak season crowds.
