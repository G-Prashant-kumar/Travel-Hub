NEW YORK

1. Statue of Liberty and Ellis Island

Symbolizing freedom and democracy, the Statue of Liberty is a must-visit landmark. Ellis Island, the historic immigration center, is nearby. Statue of Liberty Museum, Ellis Island National Museum of Immigration, and panoramic views of the NYC skyline.

2. Central Park

A sprawling urban oasis in the heart of Manhattan, offering a wide range of outdoor activities and scenic spots. Bethesda Terrace, Central Park Zoo, The Mall, Strawberry Fields, and boating on the lake.

3. Times Square

The bustling heart of Manhattan, known for its bright lights, Broadway theaters, and endless entertainment options. Broadway shows, Madame Tussauds, Ripley's Believe It or Not, and the New Year's Eve Ball Drop.
